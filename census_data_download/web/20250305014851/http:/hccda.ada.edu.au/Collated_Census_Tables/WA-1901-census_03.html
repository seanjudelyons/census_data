Description of the Boundaries (arranged in Geographical position from North to South) of the various Census Districts of Western Australia at the taking of the 7th Census, on the 31st March, 1901.

East Kimberley Census District.

All that portion of the Colony North of the 16th parallel of South latitude.

Kimberley Goldfields Census District.

Bounded on the North by the 16th parallel of South latitude. On the East by the Eastern boundary of the Colony (longitude 129° East). On the South by the parallel of 19° 30′ South latitude ; and on the West by the 126th meridian of East longitude.

West Kimberley Census District.

Bounded on the North by the sea-coast (including the islands adjacent) from a sport situated on the 123rd meridian of East longitude to a spot situated in Doubtful Bay on the 16th parallel of South latitude, and thence by the 16th parallel of South latitude. On the East by the 126th meridian of East longitude. On the South by the parallel of 19° 30′ South latitude ; and on the Westward by the 123rd meridian of East longitude.

Broome Census District.

Bounded on the South by the parallel of 19° 30′ South latitude. On the East by the 123rd meridian of East longitude ; and on the North and West by the sea-coast, including the islands adjacent.

Pllbarra Census District.

Bounded on the Westward by the left bank of the Yule River upwards to survey mark P 16 ; thence South-Easterly to trigonometrical station B 3 ; thence South to the Tropic of Capricorn, and along it East to the East boundary of the Colony. On the East by the East boundary of the Colony. On the North by a West line extending from the said East boundary of the Colony along the 19° 30′ parallel of South latitude to the sea coast ; and thence along the sea coast West-South-Westward to the mouth of the said Yule River, including all islands adjacent.

Roebourne Census District.

Bounded on the South and West by a South-South-East line from the sea coast to the trigonometrical station on the Pedamullah Hill ; thence East-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station on Mount Darnell ; thence East-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station on Mount Rica ; thence East-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station Y 13 ; thence South-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station on Mount Lionel ; thence due South to the Tropic of Capricorn, and along it East to a point due South from trigonometrical station B 3. On the East by a North line from said Tropic of Capricorn to said trigonometrical station B 3 ; thence North-Westerly to survey mark P 16, on the Yule River, and along its left bank downward to the sea coast ; and on the North by the said sea coast, including the islands adjacent.

Ashburton Census District.

Bounded on the North and East by a South-South-East line from the sea-coast to the trigonometrical station on Pedamullah Hill ; thence East-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station on Mount Darnell ; thence East-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station on Mount Rica ; thence East-South-Easterly to trigonometrical station Y 13 ; thence South-South-Easterly to the trigonometrical station on Mount Lionel ; thence due South to the Tropic of Capricorn. On the West by the sea-coast, including the islands adjacent ; and on the South by the Tropic of Capricorn.

Peak Hill Census District.

Bounded by lines starting from the summit of Mount Hale, and extending North-Westerly to the summit of Mount Gascoyne ; thence East-North-Easterly to trigonometrical station K 20 on a peak near the source of the Lyons River ; thence North-Easterly to the summit of Mount Bresnahan ; thence North to the Tropic of Capricorn ; thence East along same to the boundary of the Colony ; thence South along said boundary to the 26th parallel of South latitude ; thence West along the said parallel to a point situate North from the summit of Mount Russell ; thence South to same ; and thence North-Westerly to the starting point.

Gascoyne Census District.

Bounded by lines starting from the summit of Mount Murchison, and extending North-Easterly to the summit of Mount Hale ; thence North-Westerly to the summit of Mount Gascoyne ; thence East-North-Easterly to trigonometrical station K 20 on a peak near the source of the Lyons River ; thence West along same to the sea-coast ; thence Southerly along the coast to a point situate West from the starting point ; and thence East to the said point, including the islands adjacent.

Murchison Census District.

Bounded by lines starting from the summit of Mount Murchison and extending North-Easterly to the summit of Mount Hale ; thence South-Easterly to the summit of Mount Russell ; thence South-South-Westerly to the North-West corner of North Coolgardie Goldfield, said corner being situate about seven miles South and about three and a-quarter miles East from Wyemandoo Hill ; thence North-North-Westerly to the summit of said hill ; thence West-North-Westerly to trigonometrical station K 6 on Goonahmoudey Peak ; thence North-North-Westerly to the summit of Mount Farmer, onwards to the summit of Mount Luke, and onwards to the summit of Mount Murchison, the starting point.

East Murchison Census District.

Bounded by lines starting from the Southernmost corner of the Murchison Goldfields situate East about 4frac12; miles, and south about 4 miles from trigonometrical station K 75, on Wyemandoo Hill, and extending East to a point situate East 15 miles and North about 44 miles from the summit of Mount Ida ; thence North about 4frac14; miles ; thence East about 74frac14; miles, passing through a tree marked AN 33 at Doyle's Well, to a point situate West 2 miles 10 chains, and North about 35frac12; miles from tree marked B 82 at Brickey's Soak, thence North about 50 miles ; thence East to the 125th meridian East longitude ; thence South to a point situate East from the highest summit of the Wongan Hills ; thence East to the Eastern boundary of the Colony (129° East longitude) ; thence North along said boundary to the 26th parallel of South latitude ; thence West to a point situate North from the summit of Mount Russell ; thence South to the said summit ; thence South-South-Westward along the Eastern boundary of the Murchison Goldfields to the starting point.

Yalgoo Census District.

Bounded by lines starting from the summit of Mount Murchison and extending West-South-Westerly to the summit of Tallering Peak ; thence South-Easterly to the summit of Mugga Mugga Hill, and onwards to the summit of Mount Gibson, which lies about 12 miles South-West from Ninghan Creek ; then Eastward to trigonometrical station K 83 on the Western shore of Lake Moore ; then due East to the Western boundary of the Yilgarn Goldfield and along it North to its North-West corner ; thence West-North-Westward to the summit of Wyemandoo Hill and onwards to trigonometrical station K 6 on Goonahmoudey Peak ; thence North-West ward to the summit of Mount Farmer and onwards to the summit of Mount Luke and onwards to the summit of Mount Murchison.

Bounded by lines starting from a spot 15 miles East and 13 miles North from the summit of Mount Ida, and extending North about 35frac12; miles ; thence East about 74frac14; miles, passing through a tree marked AN 33 at Doyle's Well to a spot about 2 miles 10 chains West and about 35 miles North from tree marked B 82 at Brickey's Soak ; then North 50 miles ; then East to the 125th meridian East longitude, and South along that meridian to a spot due East of the said tree marked B 82 at Brickey's Soak, and West through the said tree to the starting point.
